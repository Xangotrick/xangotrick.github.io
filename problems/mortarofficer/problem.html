Mortar Officer

Time required: ?

Requirements:

This problem requires use of:
  • - Functions
  • - Lists
  • - Loops

Files required:

  • - mortarofficer.py

Problem:

You are an officer during WWII, and you are in charge of aiming a mortar on targets at a given distance; however you are not very good at aiming. This problem is centered around finding a way to correctly aim your mortar, while minimising the amout of shells wasted before correctly hitting your target. The target may be too far for you to reach, in which case, report the issue to your commanding officer.



Questions:

  1. Download the mortarofficer.py file that will be used for the exercise.

    2. You will answer the following questions by using the designated space in the .py file, and by running that file. target is a random integer that represents the distance of the target to hit. Your mortars must fall within 25m of the target. ballistic() is a function that fires a mortar shell: it takes an angle in degrees as a parameter, and returns the distance at which the mortar hit the ground.

    You may not change the value of target, and the only other pre-build function you may interract with is ballistic, to fire mortar shells.


  2. Write a function that makes you fire mortar shots randomly until you hit the assigned target.
  3. Write two other functions, each with a more methodical way of finding the correct angle.

    4. HQ warns you that the R.A.F. is flying over the area at a ground height of 600m. It is imperative that our mortar shells do not hit them.


  4. Adapt the three functions to comply with this demand.
  5. Each mortar shot costs 500$. On average, how much would each method cost to hit a target.
