Dichotomy

Time required: 1H

Requirements:

This problem requires use of:
  • - Functions
  • - Loops

Problem:

Bob and Jane are playing a number guessing game. Jan can choose any integer between 0 and 100. Jane can only tell Bob if his guess is correct, too high or too low. She is thinking of 15.

Bob is well versed in the dichotomy method and says: "50"

Jane replies: "Too high!"

Bob then says: "25"

Jane replies: "Too high!"

Bob then says: "12" (he would have liked to say 12.5, but the rules state that we are only guessing integers)

Jane says: "Too low!"

Bob thinks for a second, remembers that (25 - 12)/2 + 12 is equal to 18.5 and says: "18"

Jane says: "Too high!"

Bob thinks again about how (18- 12)/2 + 12 = 15 and says 15!

Jane says: "Correct!"

We will be answering the following question, today: is it better to go through Bob's method, or to randomly guess.


Questions:

  1. Write as variables the parameters of the problem: minint, maxint, inttoguess.
  2. Write a Jane function that take a parameter inputint, and returns information on whether inputint is higher, lower or equal to inttoguess.
  3. Write a function that emulates Bob's way of finding the right integer.
  4. Find the average amount of guesses needed to get the right answer through Bob's method.
  5. Write a function that emulates randomly picking an integer until getting the right answer.
  6. Find the average amount of guesses needed to get the right answer through random guesses.
